 Artificial Intelligence in Public Transit: Better, Faster, Safer?
Site Author
 Arianna Smith
Abstract
 This article explores the expanding role of artificial intelligence (AI) in California's public transportation systems.  AI is being utilized in areas such as real-time operations management, customer analytics, intelligent ticketing systems, predictive maintenance, scheduling, multimodal journey planning, disruption management, and fraud detection.  The U.S. Department of Energy and the Federal Transit Administration have funded AI projects like the Chattanooga Regional Transportation Authority's partnership with Vanderbilt University and SmartTransit AI.  This initiative aims to enhance energy efficiency and affordability in public transit through AI-driven analysis of rider demand, traffic congestion, and vehicle energy use.  The article underscores the transformative potential of AI in enhancing the efficiency, safety, and user experience of public transit systems, while also highlighting the importance of addressing associated challenges related to privacy and system security.

 The Impact of Computer Engineering on Transportation Systems
Site Author
 Grady Andersen
Abstract
 This article examines how computer engineering is transforming transportation through the development of autonomous vehicles.  Autonomous vehicles aim to reduce accidents caused by human error, which accounts for approximately 94% of traffic incidents.  The autonomous vehicle industry is projected to generate significant economic activity and create new employment opportunities.​  Technologies like LiDAR, radar, and cameras provide vehicles with accurate environmental perception.​  Building public confidence requires education, transparency, and demonstration of the technology's benefits.  Addressing unpredictable situations that autonomous vehicles may encounter is crucial for their reliability.  Computer engineering is pivotal in advancing autonomous vehicle technology, offering potential improvements in safety, efficiency, and accessibility within transportation systems.  The article also highlights the challenges that need to be addressed for successful integration to occur.
